Course Overview

Key Idea

Equipping high school students with essential academic, digital, and life skills often overlooked in standard curricula, preparing them for college-level learning and independence.

Goal

To transform students into self-reliant, critically-thinking, and emotionally intelligent individuals ready to navigate the academic and social challenges of college life.

Target Audience

High school students of whom pursuing 10th and 12th grades.

Duration

Self-Paced (8-10 weeks to cover all modules) OR Instructor-Led (40 hours of total content).

Format of course

Online, Modular, Interactive Lessons and Practical Exercises

Prerequisites

A desire to succeed in college and a willingness to learn!

Core Modules & Key Topics

Module Core Skill Focus Contents Covered
Academic Toolkit Mastering College-Level Learning Effective Note-Taking (Cornell, Mind-Mapping), Active Reading Strategies for dense textbooks, Research & Citation Basics (MLA/APA formatting), Exam Preparation (study scheduling, test-taking strategies).
Digital & Critical Literacy Navigating the Modern Information Landscape Digital Citizenship (online reputation netiquette), Information Vetting (identifying fake news), Academic Software Proficiency (cloud storage, learning management systems like Canvas/Blackboard), Data Security.
Critical Thinking & Problem Solving Developing Analytical Power Logical Reasoning (identifying fallacies), Structured Problem Solving (defining, analyzing, implementing), Argumentation & Debate Skills, Creative Thinking for project development.
Time Management Owning Your Schedule Prioritization Techniques (Eisenhower Matrix, ABC analysis), Goal Setting (SMART goals), Overcoming Procrastination, Developing a Daily/Weekly Study Rhythm (scheduling classes, study time, and social life)
Emotional Intelligence (EQ) & Wellness Building Resilience and Self-Awareness Stress Management (mindfulness, boundary setting), Self-Advocacy (communicating needs to professors/advisors), Emotional Regulation (handling academic pressure), Conflict Resolution in group settings
Foundational Coding Skills Practical Independence for College Basic Budgeting & Financial Literacy, Healthy Habits (sleep hygiene, nutrition), Campus Resource Navigation (tutoring centers, health services), Networking (connecting with peers and faculty)

What You Will Gain

  • Gaining basic programming skills: Learn the fundamentals of C and C++ programming languages.
  • Confidence & Independence: Move from being a dependent high school student to a proactive and self-directed college learner.
  • The 'Unwritten Rules' Advantage: Master the hidden academic and social expectations that trip up most first-year college students.
  • Personalized Roadmap: A clear plan for your first semester of college, minimizing culture shock and maximizing academic performance.
